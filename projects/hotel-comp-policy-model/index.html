Comp Policy Shadow-Validation Decision

A simulation-backed decision on which service-recovery policy should enter shadow validation before manager use.

Executive decision

Run Guardrailed recovery in invisible shadow mode for four weeks or at least 50 eligible cases. It was the lowest modeled-cost policy to clear every declared guardrail.

Selected policy: Guardrailed recovery · guest protection first · cost choice second · manager judgment retained

Synthetic comparison, not observed Proper Hotels performance or projected savings. Public context informs gesture fit only.

Decision evidence

Why Guardrailed Recovery advances

Five policies were tested against the same 430 synthetic recovery cases using one guardrail-first selection rule.

Material tradeoff

Compared with Intelligent Generosity, the selected policy reduces modeled midpoint cost by $9,906, increases direct-refund face-value exposure by $9,535, and reduces manager-review volume by 30.2%. These are simulated tradeoffs to validate in shadow mode, not projected business results.
