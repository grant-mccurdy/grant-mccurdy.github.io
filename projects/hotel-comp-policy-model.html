Project Brief

Hotel Comp Policy Model

An explainable manager decision product for matching a guest recovery gesture to the service failure while keeping cost assumptions, operating constraints, alternatives, and review requirements visible.

Open Decision Desk Read Decision Report GitHub Repo

Decision

Make generosity consistent and reviewable

The engine establishes a minimum recovery obligation from the incident, removes unavailable or inadequate gestures, and ranks the feasible options. Guest relationship value can add generosity but cannot lower what the service failure warrants.

The product is designed to compare transparent rules, interpretable models, nonlinear benchmarks, and manager judgment on future-dated cases before any production policy decision.

Review path

  1. Run the built-in synthetic scenario in the Decision Desk.
  2. Inspect the recommendation, alternatives, and assumptions.
  3. Read the stakeholder decision report.
  4. Review policy uncertainty and engineering lineage.
Decision analysis Sensitivity Engineering evidence

System

From scenario to accountable recommendation

Structured intake

A manager enters a synthetic incident directly or uses bounded field suggestions, then confirms the structured scenario before scoring.

Feasibility first

The engine filters unavailable gestures and enforces a recovery floor before cost or guest-relationship adjustments affect ranking.

Visible reasoning

The output includes the selected gesture, two alternatives, guest-facing value, assumed cost range, stability, approval path, and reason codes.

Validation before policy

Simulation demonstrates the contract. Property calibration would require real outcomes, temporal validation, overlap checks, manager review, and controlled shadow testing.

Current Limit

A working prototype, not a property finding

All operating records, comp history, costs, guest values, and outcomes are synthetic. Public property pages provide bounded context for guest-facing options only; the project does not claim access to internal hotel data, policy, inventory, margins, or performance.
