Content intelligence pipeline

A public-safe artifact-to-RAG workflow that turns source-adapter patterns, transcript artifacts, and OCR cleanup examples into manifests, washed information objects, retrieval-ready chunks, safety reviews, and cited analytical outputs.

View Demo Report Project Brief

System Story

From messy source artifacts to auditable RAG evidence

01

Problem

Useful decisions often live in unstructured notes, PDFs, transcript-like records, and informal planning artifacts. Without source tracking and public-safety review, generated summaries can become hard to audit and risky to publish.

02

Solution

I built a reproducible scaffold that preserves source boundaries from the start: adapter outputs become manifest records, cleaned text becomes normalized artifacts, every passage becomes a citation-preserving segment, and retrieval records carry the metadata a RAG service needs to answer from evidence.

03

Tools and Methods

The demo uses dependency-light Python, synthetic text sources, staged transcript cleanup, OCR cleanup simulation, checksum-backed manifests, whitespace normalization, deterministic segmentation, RAG index generation, public-safety review, keyword retrieval, and static HTML/Markdown outputs.

04

Result and Value

The current pipeline turns synthetic source documents and cleaned transcript/OCR examples into validated information objects, enrichment packets, retrievable segments, RAG index records, method packs, and cited demo reports without exposing private transcripts, course records, or institutional material.

05

What I Learned

The privacy boundary has to be designed into the pipeline, not patched onto the final answer. Source IDs, citation labels, public-safety review, and generated/private file separation make the workflow easier to review and safer to serve through RAG.
