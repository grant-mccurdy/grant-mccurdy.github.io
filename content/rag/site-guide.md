# Portfolio Helper Site Guide

This is a public-safe routing guide for the homepage portfolio helper. Use it to
answer visitor questions about where to start, which demos to open, and when to
send a visitor to the Data Lab.

## Where To Start

For a first-time visitor, recommend this path:

1. Start on the homepage to understand the overall systems-portfolio framing.
2. Open the Hotel Comp decision brief or live Decision Desk for the clearest
   business decision-support example.
3. Open Assessment Analytics for a fast interactive data-product demo.
4. Open the Education Data Lab for structured analytic chat over the synthetic
   education warehouse.
5. Read the Assessment Intelligence or Education Data Simulation Engine project
   page for the strongest data and SQL evidence.
6. Read Content Intelligence and try its live RAG surface for source-grounded
   retrieval evidence.
7. Read Assessment Growth Analytics in R for expected-growth modeling,
   section signal diagnostics, validation, and executive-facing R reporting.

## Visitor Intent Routing

If the visitor asks about data systems, SQL, warehouses, or synthetic education
data, recommend:

- Education Data Simulation Engine;
- Assessment Intelligence;
- Education Data Lab;
- Assessment Analytics.

If the visitor asks about dashboards, reporting, or analytics products,
recommend:

- Hotel Comp Policy Model for operational decision support;
- Assessment Analytics;
- Assessment Intelligence;
- Assessment Growth Analytics in R;
- the Assessment Intelligence project brief for the consolidated delivery story.

If the visitor asks about statistical modeling, R, expected growth, BOY/EOY
improvement, section performance, validation, or model cards, recommend:

- Assessment Growth Analytics in R;
- Assessment Intelligence for related analytics/reporting evidence.

If the visitor asks about AI workflows, feedback, review, or remediation,
recommend:

- Instructional AI Workflows;
- Assessment-to-Remediation Pipeline;
- their project briefs for the problem, approach, result, and limits.

If the visitor asks about RAG, source grounding, artifact conversion, corpus
construction, or content pipelines, recommend:

- Content Intelligence;
- the Content Intelligence RAG demo;
- the public RAG/backend project context when cited by the helper sources.

If the visitor asks about hospitality, service recovery, policy comparison,
simulation, or business decision support, recommend:

- Hotel Comp Policy Model decision brief;
- Hotel Decision Desk;
- Engineering evidence and simulation audit within that project.

## Data Lab Handoff

The homepage helper answers questions about Grant, the site, projects, demos,
and where to navigate. It should not perform statistical analysis itself.

For computational questions over the synthetic education data, send the visitor
to the Education Data Lab at:

https://grant-mccurdy.github.io/data-lab.html

Examples of Data Lab questions include:

- "How does average observed growth change by school year?"
- "Which course track has the strongest readiness pattern?"
- "Compare attendance categories by growth."
- "Show a chart of readiness by school year."
- "Which sections have the highest or lowest values?"

The homepage helper may summarize what the Data Lab is for, but actual
education-data computation belongs in the Data Lab.

## Helper Answer Style

The helper should answer concisely, cite public sources, and offer one useful
next page or demo when appropriate. It should avoid long generic summaries when
a direct route or project recommendation answers the visitor's question.

If the public corpus does not support a claim, the helper should say what is
missing rather than inventing details.
